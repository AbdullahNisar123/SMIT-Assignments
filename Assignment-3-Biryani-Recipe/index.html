Ingredients:

  • For the Rice:
    1. 2 cups basmati rice
    2. 4 cups water
    3. 1 bay leaf
    4. 3-4 cloves
    5. 1 cinnamon stick
    6. Salt (to taste)
  • For the chicken marination:
    1. 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
    2. 1 cup yogurt
    3. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    4. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    5. 2 teaspoons red chili powder
    6. 1 teaspoon garam masala
    7. Salt (to taste)
  • For cooking:
    • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
    • 3 tomatoes (chopped)
    • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
    • 1/2 cup mint leaves (chopped)
    • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
    • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon saffron (soaked in 2 tablespoons milk)
    • 1/2 teaspoon biryani masala (optional)

Recipe:

  1. Prepare the rice:
    • Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes.
    • Boil water in a large pot, add the bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, and salt.
    • Add the soaked rice and cook until 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set aside.
  2. Marinate the chicken:
    • In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
    • Add the chicken pieces and coat well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Fry the onions:
    • In a large pan, heat oil or ghee and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Set aside some fried onions for garnish.
  4. Cook the chicken:
    • In the same pan, add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
    • Add the marinated chicken and cook for about 10-15 minutes until the chicken is tender.
    • Add chopped tomatoes, coriander leaves, mint leaves, and green chilies. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Layering the biryani:
    • In a large pot, layer half of the semi-cooked rice at the bottom.
    • Add the cooked chicken mixture on top of the rice.
    • Layer the remaining rice on top of the chicken.
    • Drizzle saffron milk and sprinkle fried onions on top.
  6. Final cooking (Dum):
    • Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes (Dum cooking).
    • Once done, gently fluff the rice with a fork.
  7. Serve:
    • Garnish with more fried onions, coriander, and serve with raita.

Enjoy your delicious biryani!
